Who We Are

CSTS Health Care Inc. is a Canadian technology company based in Toronto, founded in 2013. Our mission is to transform the field of personalized medicine through a new understanding of genetically driven diseases leveraging artificial intelligence technologies and computational and systems biology technics.

Since inception, working alongside expert collaborators across various healthcare domains, we have integrated knowledge from multiple disciplines of science and created a novel digital molecular model of genetically driven disease. Combining our new understanding of genetically driven disease with our artificial intelligence technologies, we have created a new artificial intelligence driven platform called Aiomic .

Whether you are a clinical healthcare provider treating cancer, or a Next Generation Sequencing provider performing tests, or a pharmaceutical investing into new molecularly targeted agents, the Aiomic platform provides a revolutionary interpretation of Omics information to increase productivity, deepen analytical capabilities, and accelerate decision making.

Clinical Need: The treatment of Cancer requires the integration of knowledge from multiple scientific disciplines, often beyond the reach of single physicians. In addition, the volume of available information in the form of new discoveries and technology advances comes at an overwhelming rate. Consequently, the timely extraction of relevant insight and its synthesis into clinically actionable, personalised therapies, remain one of Oncologists’ biggest challenges.

Aiomic Solution: The Artificial Intelligence enabled platform Aiomic assists Oncologists in designing high quality personalised therapies. Based on a thermodynamic interpretation of the cancer micro-environment, proprietary algorithms identify the highest value target(s) in the cancer molecular signalling network, to assist doctors in personalized therapy design and drug selection. We combine this with the power of Artificial Intelligence and a unique computational cancer model to extract comprehensive information from relevant literature and deliver superior data analytics and clinical support recommendations – inhibiting disease progression and improving patient quality of life and outcome.

Pharma Need: Clinical trials carry high degree of outcome uncertainty for both patients and trial sponsors. Consequently, precious time is often invested by the patients for uncertain incremental health benefit while considerable resources are consummed by trial sponsors, often undermining the economic viability of the drug development process.

Aiomic Solution: Aiomic can determine the degree of alignment between the highest value target(s) in the cancer molecular signalling network of each individual patient and the biological target of the drug being tested. Trial sponsors can therefore select patients for trials with higher confidence in a positive outcome. This allows sponsors to save time and money, reduce exposure to Type II error, increase efficiency of the drug development process and favourably distinguish themselves in the patients’ perception.

Our Therapy Design Strategy Works

  1. Biopsy of the tumor is taken and sent for genetic analysis.
  2. A variety of Omics tests can be performed, including genetic panels, or gene expression levels.
  3. Our software takes this information and cross-references it against a large array of authoritative and vetted sources.
  4. Additional, gene expression levels are compared to a reference genome and protein-protein interaction network where a thermodynamic interpretation is applied.
  5. This thermodynamic interpretation reduces the complexity of the PPI network, and allows the clinician to focus in on a subnetwork of focus that is likely supporting the tumor.
  6. A topological measure is then used to select molecules within the subnetwork, whose targeting would most likely disrupt the cancer’s growth.
  7. These molecules are additionally compared to drug databases to identify appropriate available drugs.
  8. The RNASeq (mutational) genetic information is used to identify the variant of the mutation, and is used to filter out drug selections which are known to be ineffective.
  9. This information then updates the identified PPI subnetworks, and is used to select the optimal molecular targets and drug combinations that are most likely to be effective for the patient.
